Main Structure Equation

Core Structure

Quantum Origami models the universe as an assembly of folded two-dimensional energy sheets. Each sheet \(P_i\) carries a finite crease pattern \(C_i\) and signed fold angles \(\Theta_i\). It is folded into three-dimensional space by a map \(F_i\) and positioned by a rigid motion \(T_i\).

\[ \boxed{ C = \bigcup_{i=1}^{N} T_i\!\left(F_i(P_i)\right) } \]

This single equation defines the global geometric configuration of the universe in the Quantum Origami framework.

Structural Components

Symbol Definition Role
\(P_i\) Two-dimensional energy sheet in \(\mathbb{R}^2\) Base geometric object
\(C_i\) Finite crease pattern on \(P_i\) Defines fold geometry
\(\Theta_i\) Signed fold-angle vector \((\Theta_{i,1},\dots,\Theta_{i,K_i})\) Controls curvature, mass, and motif type
\(F_i(P_i)\) Folded three-dimensional module in \(\mathbb{R}^3\) Rigidly folded shape in space
\(T_i\) Rigid motion in \(SE(3)\) Placement of module in the universe
\(C\) Global configuration \(\bigcup_i T_i(F_i(P_i))\) The entire universe

Conceptual Flow Graph

P_i  ──►  (creases C_i, angles Θ_i)  ──►  F_i(P_i)  ──►  T_i(F_i(P_i))  ──►  C

This graph shows how sheets become modules through folding, and how modules assemble into the global configuration \(C\) through rigid placement.

Why the Main Structure Equation Is Enough

All of these quantities—mass, charge, forces, curvature, entropy—are derived from the geometry of the folds and placements. Because the physics is encoded directly in the crease patterns \(C_i\), angle vectors \(\Theta_i\), folding maps \(F_i\), and rigid motions \(T_i\), the entire universe reduces to the single structural equation

\[ C = \bigcup_{i=1}^{N} T_i(F_i(P_i)). \]

A reader can reconstruct the full Quantum Origami framework from this section: the objects, the geometry, and the emergent physics are all contained in the tables, graphs, and equations above.
