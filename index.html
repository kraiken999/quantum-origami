Main Structure Equation (Quantum Origami)

CORE STRUCTURE

In Quantum Origami, the entire universe is constructed from flat two-dimensional energy sheets \(P_i\), each carrying a finite crease pattern and signed fold angles. Each sheet is rigidly folded by a map

\[ F_i : P_i \rightarrow \mathbb{R}^3, \]

and placed in three-dimensional space by a rigid motion

\[ T_i \in SE(3). \]

The global configuration of the universe is simply the union of all folded and placed modules:

\[ \boxed{ C = \bigcup_{i=1}^{N} T_i\!\left(F_i(P_i)\right) } \]

Why the main structure equation is this simple

Quantum Origami is a geometric theory, not a field theory. There are no gauge fields, potentials, or covariant derivatives. All physical quantities—mass, curvature, forces, matter, antimatter—emerge directly from the geometry of the folds themselves. Because the physics is encoded in the crease patterns, fold angles, and rigid placements, the universe reduces to a single structural equation:

\[ C = \bigcup_{i=1}^{N} T_i(F_i(P_i)). \]

This one expression captures the entire architecture of the universe: every sheet, every fold, every module, and every interaction arises from this geometric assembly.
